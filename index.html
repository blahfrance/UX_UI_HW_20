Introduction

The team’s goal was to redesign a non-profit’s organization website. Because of the nature of non-profit’s, many of the designers are volunteers. We wanted to use this opportunity to invigorate a good cause with a useful and inviting website.

Who we chose:

When considering websites that our team wanted to redesign, we steered in the direction towards philanthropic-based organizations. We ultimately landed on the organization who we felt like needed the most updated design and functionality: Discovery Arts.

Who is Discovery Arts?

Discovery Arts, an orange county based organization, that brings art to children in hospitals who suffer from cancer and blood disorder, has a compelling mission, and we believe their impact can be stronger with a great web presence. http://www.discoveryarts.org.

The Problem
Through redlining and a heuristic evaluation, we determined what our team thought were the Discovery Arts site biggest pain points. We observed that it was hard to find out what they do. The mission statement and relevant information was scattered throughout many pages. Image quality was also a problem as well as layout hierarchy. Perhaps the biggest problem was the donation flow as there were multiple donation buttons and multiple campaigns. The site's navigation used theater terms which our team thought would not be intuitive to users and we wanted to update the logo.
The User Flows

We thought about what user flows are most important to a non-profit. We settled on 2 flows: learning what Discovery Arts is about, and donating to their cause.

User Research & Findings

We conducted 6 interviews and has 23 survey responses.

We focused on asking questions about user's donating motivations, what information is wanted before deciding to donate, and how the Discovery Art's website could be better.

Testing & Iterations
A/B Testing

We created a new logo, wrote a revised mission statement, came up with a color palettes, hero images, and typography options. We then tested them among our peers.

Style Guide

We settled on classic primary colors and organic shapes to allude to paint. For typography, we chose Bebas Neue for its modern and bold feel. Then we paired it with Adobe Caslon to soften and balance the heavier typeface.
